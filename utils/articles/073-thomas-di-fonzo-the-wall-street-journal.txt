What Can You Buy With Bitcoin: A $10 Pizza for $76?
A video on the challenges of spending the virtual currency on the streets of New York
Bitcoin is a virtual currency, but very few people use it to actually pay for things because of transaction fees and its rising value. WSJ's Thomas Di Fonzo takes to the streets of New York to try to spend bitcoin at brick-and-mortar establishments. Watch the above video without a subscription.
By
Thomas Di Fonzo and Alexander Hotz
Dec. 13, 2017 1:00 p.m. ET
One of the main reasons bitcoin was introduced was to make digital payments more efficient. But with the value of bitcoin seeming to rise every day, most people are holding on to it and using it more as a store of value, like gold, instead of actually using it as a currency. and only exchanging it for cash when they need it.
In this video, we set out on the streets of New York to see how we could spend bitcoin at brick-and-mortar businesses. Watch to see what we found out.
