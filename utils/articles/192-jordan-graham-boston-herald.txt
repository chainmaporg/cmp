‘NOW A FRENZY’: Secretary of State William F. Galvin warns investors that the cryptocurrency bitcoin, which has rocketed up by more than 2,000 percent in the past year, is ‘particularly speculative, there is nothing behind it.’
comments
The warnings against banking on red-hot bitcoin are adding up, with state and federal officials saying the volatile digital currency is “highly speculative” and dangerous for investors who don’t understand what they are putting their money into.
“We’re fearful that many people may not understand it and may lose money,” said Secretary of State William F. Galvin. “We believe it’s particularly speculative, there is nothing behind it.”
